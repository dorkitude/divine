A miser buried his gold in a hole and visited it daily, never spending a coin. A thief stole it. A neighbor said: "You might as well bury a stone and visit it — for all the use you made of your gold, it might as well have been one."

**Moral:** Wealth that is never used has no value. Possessions only matter when they serve a purpose.
