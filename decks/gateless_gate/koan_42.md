The Buddha found a girl sitting in deep meditation near his seat. Manjusri, the bodhisattva of wisdom, could not rouse her. A lesser bodhisattva snapped his fingers, and she emerged.

Why could the wisest being not do what a simple one could? Wisdom sometimes overthinks the simplest things.
