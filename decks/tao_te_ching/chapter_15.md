The ancient masters were subtle, profound, and penetrating — too deep to be understood. Because they cannot be understood, we can only describe their appearance: cautious, like crossing a frozen river; alert, like warriors in enemy territory; courteous, like visiting guests; yielding, like ice about to melt; simple, like uncarved wood. Who can wait quietly while the mud settles? Who can remain still until the moment of action?
