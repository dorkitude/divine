"If anyone tells you that someone has spoken ill of you, do not make excuses about what is said — instead respond: they must not know my other faults, or they would not have mentioned only these."

— Epictetus, *Enchiridion*, 33

When you refuse to be wounded, the weapon becomes useless. Humor disarms what defensiveness amplifies.
