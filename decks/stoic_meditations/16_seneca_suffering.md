"We suffer more often in imagination than in reality. There are more things likely to frighten us than there are to crush us."

— Seneca, *Letters to Lucilius*, 13

Most of your suffering is anticipatory. The feared event, when it actually arrives, is almost always more manageable than the dread that preceded it.
